Group acceptance criteria for each of the user stories

  1. As a student, I wish to login to the library system so that I can access online services.
  • When I enter my valid student ID and password, I should be successfully logged into the library system.
  • If I enter invalid credentials, I should receive an error message indicating the login failure.
  • Once logged in, I should have access to all online services provided by the library.

Start Date: April 23 2024

End Date: April 25 2024

2.  As a student, I wish to search for a particular book based on its title so that I can find it in the library.

  • When I enter the title of a book into the search bar, the system should return a list of books matching the entered title.
  • If there are no matches, the system should display a message indicating that no books were found.
  • Each search result should include the title, author, availability status, and location of the book in the library.

Start Date: April 302024

End Date: May 05 2024

 

3. As a student, I wish to search for all books held for a particular author so that I can view their details.

  • When I enter the name of an author into the search bar, the system should return a list of books written by that author.
  • If there are no books by the specified author, the system should display a message indicating that no books were found.
  • Each search result should include the title, author, availability status, and location of the book in the library.

Start Date: May 06 2024

End Date: June 13 2024

4. As a student I wish to put a hold on a particular book so that I can borrow it.

  • When I find the book I want to borrow, I should be able to select it and choose the "Hold" option.
  • If the book is available, the system should confirm my hold request and notify me of the expected pickup date.
  • If the book is currently checked out by another user, the system should allow me to place a hold and notify me when the book becomes available.
  • After successfully placing a hold, I should receive a confirmation message with details of the book and the hold status.

Start Date: June 14, 2024

End Date: June 24 2024

Factors in the agile approach that would ensure the cost of change in a project is minimised.

Several aspects in an Agile methodology contribute to the reduction of change costs in a project:
Iterative development is emphasized by agile approaches like Scrum and Kanban. By decomposing the project into smaller chunks, modifications may be implemented at each iteration, hence mitigating the risk and cost involved with making changes towards the end of the development cycle.
Continuous Feedback: Agile methodology encourages the consistent and ongoing input from stakeholders, such as customers and end-users, at every stage of the development process. Receiving input at an early stage and on a regular basis enables making appropriate improvements, hence decreasing the probability of expensive rework in the future.
Agile techniques promote adaptability over inflexible planning. Agile teams readily accept and are ready to modify project objectives and priorities in response to changing client needs or market conditions. Teams can swiftly adapt to changes without incurring substantial extra expenses due to this flexibility.
Cross-functional collaboration refers to the practice of assembling agile teams that include of people who possess a wide range of skills and knowledge. The collaborative atmosphere fosters efficient communication and problem-solving, allowing teams to effectively address change and reduce the impact on project costs.
Test-Driven Development (TDD) is a practice in Agile development that promotes the writing of tests before producing code. This methodology facilitates the early detection of flaws during the development phase, hence diminishing the expenses associated with rectifying problems that emerge as a result of subsequent alterations.
Continuous Integration and Delivery (CI/CD): Agile teams commonly embrace CI/CD approaches to automate the process of incorporating code changes and releasing software updates. Automation decreases the duration and exertion needed to execute modifications, hence decreasing the expenses linked to change management.
In general, the Agile methodology promotes a culture of flexibility, cooperation, and ongoing enhancement, all of which work together to reduce the expenses associated with making changes in a project.

Importance of a sprint review meeting in the SCRUM approach.

The Sprint Review meeting is an essential element of the Scrum system, and its significance is derived from many fundamental factors:

The Sprint Review allows the Scrum Team to present the progress made during the sprint to stakeholders, such as the Product Owner and other relevant individuals. This display of gradual advancement promotes openness and guarantees synchronization between the development team and stakeholders.
Collecting Feedback: The Sprint Review promotes active engagement from stakeholders, who may offer useful input on the given increment. This input serves to confirm assumptions, clarify needs, and pinpoint prospective enhancements or alterations to the product. By integrating stakeholder input at the initial stages and throughout the development process, the Scrum Team can guarantee that the product aligns with the changing requirements of its consumers.

The Sprint Review provides the Scrum Team with a chance to examine the product increment and evaluate its compliance with the Definition of Done (DoD). If any problems or obstacles are found during the evaluation, the team may openly discuss them and work together to decide on the best course of action. The process of examining and adjusting allows the team to consistently enhance and provide superior increments in succeeding sprints.
The Sprint Review promotes accountability within the Scrum Team by offering a platform to assess the team's performance and advancement towards accomplishing sprint objectives. Through the process of examining the finished work and engaging in discussions about any deviations from the original plan, team members may pinpoint areas that require improvement and implement necessary corrective measures.
The Sprint Review facilitates cooperation and communication among stakeholders, developing a collective comprehension of the product vision and objectives. The conference fosters healthy debate and agreement on goals by assembling individuals with varied viewpoints, ultimately leading to the success of the project.
The Sprint Review meeting is essential in the Scrum framework as it promotes openness, collects input, facilitates inspection and adaption, ensures accountability, and encourages collaboration among stakeholders.
